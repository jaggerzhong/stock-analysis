# Serenity Chokepoint Framework

This framework is an architecture-first overlay for stock research. It is not a valuation replacement and not a direct trading signal.

## Core Thesis

Large mispricings can appear when the market still prices a company using an old category while the next system architecture makes one of its products, assets, or capabilities a production bottleneck.

The useful question is not only "is this cheap?" but:

1. What new architecture is being built?
2. Which production step becomes scarce or hard to replace?
3. Which company controls that step?
4. What evidence proves the relationship?
5. What will force institutions to reprice it?

## Research Order

1. Architecture shift: identify the next infrastructure stack involved.
2. Chokepoint: test whether supply is tight, substitution is hard, and market understanding is low.
3. Evidence tiering: separate confirmed facts from reported links, ecosystem mapping, and inference.
4. Category correction: check whether the market is valuing the company as the wrong business type.
5. Financial validation: look for capacity, prepayment, guidance, qualification, volume orders, pipeline growth, or margin targets.
6. Rough sizing: estimate revenue build, BOM value, forward multiple, or upstream/downstream market-cap mismatch.
7. Capital flow: check whether index inclusion, analyst coverage, large holders, ADR/Nasdaq access, or liquidity can unlock repricing.
8. Negative screen: penalize toxic ATM, heavy dilution, float unlocks, logo-only partnerships, weak contracts, or retail-exit financing.
9. Conviction tier: label the idea as high-conviction, research thesis, watchlist, or info-only.

## Evidence Tiers

| Tier | Meaning | Required Wording |
|------|---------|------------------|
| 1 Confirmed | Company disclosure, public order, official partnership | confirmed / disclosed |
| 2 Reported | Sell-side, media, or supply-chain reporting | reported by / according to |
| 3 Ecosystem Mapping | Adjacent supplier, customer, or technology fit | mapped to / inferred ecosystem |
| 4 Inference | Analyst judgment or model assumption | I infer / likely / hypothesis |

Never promote tier 3 or tier 4 evidence into tier 1 language.

## Scorecard

Each dimension is scored 0-5:

| Dimension | 0 | 5 |
|-----------|---|---|
| Architecture relevance | No clear architecture shift | Directly tied to next-gen infrastructure |
| Chokepoint scarcity | Commodity or many substitutes | Tight supply and hard replacement |
| Market misunderstanding | Fully understood | Misclassified or ignored |
| Evidence quality | Mostly inference | Confirmed or strong reported evidence |
| Financial validation | No path to revenue | Capacity/orders/guidance validates ramp |
| Capital-flow unlock | No obvious buyer base | Clear institutional/index/liquidity catalyst |
| Negative screen | Severe dilution/structure risk | Clean structure or justified financing |

Suggested interpretation:

- 28-35: high-conviction candidate if market regime and valuation allow.
- 21-27: research thesis; verify evidence and financial path before sizing.
- 14-20: watchlist only.
- 0-13: info-only or avoid unless facts materially improve.

## How This Fits The Existing Stock Skill

Use this framework before the normal value/risk engine when the user asks about AI infrastructure, supply-chain bottlenecks, thematic re-rating, chokepoints, or whether a company is being misclassified.

Then run the standard stock-analysis workflow for market regime, data quality, valuation, technical timing, and position sizing. The Serenity overlay can raise research priority, but it does not bypass data-quality gates or portfolio risk limits.
